HS Kwon*, Z. Yu, X. Lee, E. M. Dede, and T. Lee, <i><u>Advanced Materials</u></i>, 2026
[[Publication]](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adma.202519545){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}

A flexible and lightweight acoustic metamaterial composed of air cavities encased in polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) is introduced to enhance ultrasound power transfer. The metamaterial leverages the strong reflectivity of air cavity boundaries to concentrate incoming ultrasound, thereby improving the performance of piezoelectric energy harvesters. Experimental demonstration of a metamaterial enhanced ultrasound energy harvester (Meta-UEH) showed more than 100% increase in harvested power while maintaining robustness to deformation.
