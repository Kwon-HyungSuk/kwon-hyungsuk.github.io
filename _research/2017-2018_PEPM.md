TEXT

Figure 1
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure1.jpg'>
The PEPM enhances sound pressure with impedance contrast to background fluid. To minimize the insertion loss, impedance was gradually increased by 0.5 &middot; z<sub>air</sub> per unit cell. Quarter of the metamaterial is removed for visibility.

Figure 2
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure2.jpg'>
(a) Unit cell dimensions. The 45 degree ramp is added to the perforated plate for fused filament fabrication 3D printing. (b) Acoustic impedance and refractive index of a unit cell versus perforation radius. Radii correspond to multiples of 0.5 &middot; z<sub>air</sub> are selected for PEPM design. (c) Impedance variation around the center frequency.

Figure 3
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure3.jpg'>
(a) Finite element model of the PEPM and the simulation setup. (b) The simulated acoustic pressure distribution for incident sound of 3 kHz showed increasing sound pressure level while forming a standing wave due to the wall at the end. The pressure was normalized by the incident wave pressure. (c) The simulated pressure enhancement in the band 100 Hz-7000 Hz.

Figure 4
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure4.jpg'>
(a) Photo of the metamaterial cross section showing inclusions inside a cylindrical waveguide. A microphone covered the end of the PEPM to measure enhanced sounds. (b) Gaussian pulses measured after propagating through the PEPM and an empty waveguide. (c) Pressure amplitude spectrum of the two measured signals. (d) The measured pressure enhancement in the band 300 Hz-6000 Hz.

Figure 5
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure5.jpg'>
(a) Pressure enhancement was relatively constant over the target frequency range. This even increment allowed enhancing Gaussian pulses without distorting the shape of signals. (b) Simulated pressure enhancements for different unit cell sizes in the propagation direction.
