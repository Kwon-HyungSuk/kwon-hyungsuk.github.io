Figure 1

<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure1.jpg'>
Ultrasonic beam forming and steering device. (a) The reconfigurable metamaterial lens transforms cylindrical waves from a line source into a collimated beam and continuously steers the beam. (b) The phase advance (<i>Φ</i>) through the lens is composed of a constant phase advance (<i>Φ</i>') needed to realize the collimated beam and a variable phase advance needed for steering (<i>∆Φ</i>). (c) Phase advance (<i>Φ</i>) for beam forming and steering.

Figure 2
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure2.jpg'>
Unit cell design. (a) Unit cell composed of a top (fixed) component and a bottom (mobile) component. The bottom component moves vertically to tune the acoustic properties of the unit cell by changing the perforation size <i>d</i>. (b) Effective refractive index and relative impedance of a unit cell for different values of the perforation size <i>d</i>.

Figure 3
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure3.jpg'>
Reconfigurable metamaterial design. (a) Lens geometry obtained by periodically replicating the unit cell shown in Figure 2(a). The bottom component (highlighted in purple) has the flexibility to change shape to implement the prescribed refractive index. In this implementation, the component gradient changes only in the transverse direction <i>y</i>, i.e. the cell perforation size <i>d</i> is the same for cells characterized by a constant <i>y</i>. (b) Lens cross section simulation for selected values of parameter <i>d</i>. Wavelength inside the lens varies depending on <i>d</i>. (c) Phase and amplitude of transmitted wave versus <i>d</i>. (d) Ideal geometry profiles for the bottom component along the red dotted line shown in Figure 3(a) for beam forming and steering at 0°, 15° and 20°. Simple rotation of the bottom component designed for 0° steering results in good approximations of the ideal geometries of the bottom component up to 15° steering angles.

Figure 4
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure4.jpg'>
Experimental setup. (a) Photo of the experimental setup showing an omnidirectional transducer that couples cylindrical waves to the input aperture of the lens through a parallel plate waveguide. The ultrasound processed by the lens is measured in an area surrounding the lens output aperture (highlighted in red). (b) Metamaterial structure showing the fixed element (top panel), mobile element (middle panel) and the assembled lens (bottom panel) showing the slit through which sound enters the metamaterial (input aperture) The inset shows a zoomed-in region of the microstructure to enhance visibility. (c) Acoustic pressure fields measured in front of the lens for 0° (left) and 15° (right) steering angles. The measured field contained inside the highlighted rectangles were propagated numerically using the Green’s function method.

Figure 5
======
<img src='/images/research/2017-2018_PEPM/figure5.jpg'>
Far-field performance measured in a 3 kHz bandwidth. The measured far-field pressure distributions shows that the device forms highly directive ultrasound beams with small side lobes at the representative angles of (a) 0° and (b) 15°.The pressure amplitude in the presence of the lens is up to 20 dB larger than without the lens.
