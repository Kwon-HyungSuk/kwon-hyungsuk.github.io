HS Kwon and B.-I. Popa\*, <i><u>The Journal of the Acoustical Society of America</u></i>, vol. 145, no.6, pp. 3633-3639, 2019
[[Publication]](https://pubs.aip.org/asa/jasa/article-abstract/145/6/3633/940689/Design-and-experimental-demonstration-of-broadband?redirectedFrom=fulltext){:target="_blank" rel="noopener"}

An acoustic pressure–enhancing passive metafluid (PEPM) consisting of an array of unit cells with gradually increasing acoustic impedances is introduced. The PEPM enhances incoming sound by increasing acoustic pressure while reducing particle velocity. The gradually increasing impedance enables nearly uniform acoustic pressure enhancement over a broad frequency range while minimizing waveform distortion, making the PEPM ideal for improving the sensitivity of MEMS microphones.
